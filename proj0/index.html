01

Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Exploring how distance and zoom affect portrait distortion

Method

The Wrong Way

Take a close-up selfie portrait, resulting in typical distortion

The Right Way

Step back several feet, zoom in, maintain same face size

Results

Close-up Portrait
(Distorted)

VS

Zoomed Portrait
(Natural)

Analysis

Here, I took a picture of Joy from really close. This resulted in a distorted image that makes her facial features look a bit disproportional. After moving back and zooming in, I took the picture on the right. In this picture, we can see that this new portrait captures a more natural perspective of her features.

02

Architectural Perspective Compression

Demonstrating how zoom affects depth perception in urban scenes

Method

Zoomed Shot

Take a zoomed photo down a long street or path

Walk Closer

Move closer, take unzoomed photo with similar framing

Results

Compressed View
(Far with Zoom)

VS

Natural Depth
(Closer, No Zoom)

Analysis

Here, I took a picture of an area on campus near the Valley Life Sciences Building. As you can see, the path looks different in the compressed view picture. Also, in that picture, the scene looks flatter. However, if we look at the picture taken closer and with no zoom, we can see the features of the path (like the lines on the floor) and even the bike racks, which are hidden by the perspective of the compressed view.
